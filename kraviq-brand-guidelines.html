Brand Story

Who we are, why we exist, and what we stand for.

Kraviq was built by a forex trader who was tired of running his trading business from spreadsheets, scattered journals, and gut feeling. The vision is simple: give every retail forex trader the infrastructure that professional trading firms take for granted. Section 02

Logo System

The Kraviq mark, wordmark, and all approved combinations.

The Mark — Concept

The Kraviq mark is a unified symbol of K and Q — two letters sharing one form. The Q provides the outer circular arc. The K lives inside, its strokes filling the circle with precision. The Q's tail descends bottom-right, completing the letterform. K and Q are not placed side by side. They share the same space — one mark, two letters, one identity.

Ghost White #F8F8FF vs Pure White #FFFFFF: Pure white on dark backgrounds creates harsh contrast. Ghost White has a subtle blue undertone (255 in blue channel) that harmonizes with the primary blue and reduces eye strain. This is the same technique used by Linear, Vercel, and Notion. Never substitute #FFFFFF.

Typography Rules

Core Rules

Headlines use Space Grotesk. Body text uses Inter. Numbers always use JetBrains Mono. These rules have no exceptions.

Tight tracking on display text (−0.02 to −0.03em) makes headlines read as one confident unit.

Never use font weights below 300 at sizes below 14px — unreadable on dark backgrounds.

Data Rules

Line length: 60–75 characters maximum for body text blocks.

Financial numbers: always monospace, always right-aligned in tables, always show + or − sign explicitly.

The tagline uses Inter Light 300 to contrast with the Space Grotesk Bold wordmark. Weight difference creates visual hierarchy.

Section 08

Usage Rules

What to do. What never to do. The rules that protect the brand.

✓ Do

✓Use the mark on dark backgrounds (#0A0A0F, #111118) as primary usage
✓Keep minimum clear space of 1× mark height on all sides
✓Use Ghost White (#F8F8FF) — never pure #FFFFFF
✓Keep the q accent always #4F6EF7
✓Use monospace font for all financial numbers
✓Maintain profit/loss color meanings always
✓Use the full logo (mark + wordmark) as the primary identifier
✓Scale mark proportionally — always maintain aspect ratio

✗ Never Do

✗Change the q accent to any other color
✗Stretch, rotate, or distort the mark in any way
✗Use pure white (#FFFFFF) instead of Ghost White (#F8F8FF)
✗Place the logo on busy or photographic backgrounds
✗Add drop shadows, glows, or raster effects to the logo
✗Use profit green (#22C55E) for anything except profit/success
✗Use loss red (#EF4444) for anything except loss/danger/error
✗Use the logo below minimum sizes (24px mark, 120px full)

Background Usage

File Formats

When to use each format and how to export correctly from Illustrator.

FormatUse CaseWhen to Use
SVGPrimary digital formatWeb, app, email, Figma. Use everywhere possible. Scales infinitely.
PNG @1xGeneral digital useDocuments, presentations, Slack, when SVG not supported
PNG @2xRetina displaysApp icons, high-DPI screens. Always provide alongside @1x
PNG @3xHigh resolutionApp Store assets, very large display, marketing materials
WebPWebsite performanceReplace PNG on web. 25-35% smaller. Generate via squoosh.app
PDFPrint + sharingPrint materials, sharing with printers, editable format
AIMaster sourceNever delete. Never share publicly. Illustrator edits only.
ICOBrowser favicon onlyMust include 16, 32, 48px. Generate at realfavicongenerator.net

Export Checklist

BEFORE EXPORTING ANY LOGO FILE

□All text converted to outlines
□All strokes expanded
□No raster effects applied
□No linked images (all embedded)
□Correct artboard size
□Tested at minimum sizes (16px)
□Tested in monochrome — still reads
□.ai master saved before export
